Help

Welcome to the Ocean Sampling Day App!

The OSD App is specially designed for people who like to participate in the Ocean Sampling Day event which will take place on summer solstice June 21st, 2014. With this App you can enter your measured data, observations and photos of the oceans and seas directly into your phone and send it to the OSD App Server to make it publicly available for everyone.

Screens and Buttons

Personal

In the 'Personal' area you can save your personal information. This information will be stored on your phone and transferred together with your collected data to the OSD App Server. You can change this information whenever and as often as you like, just don't forget to click the 'Save' button at the bottom of the screen. If you participate in the Ocean Sampling Day please choose Micro B3 as the project. The Project, First name and Last name are mandatory fields. Remember, that the name you enter here will be displayed with the data you upload on the OSD App Server.

New Sample

When you hit 'New Sample' a blank new sample form will open. Here you can enter all your data. Mandatory fields are marked with red stars.To save your entries you have to go to the last page and hit the 'Done' button. Then the sample data will be saved locally on your phone and can be displayed and further edited using the 'Show Samples' option.

Show Samples

Here you can view a list of all your data you have entered so far. Green samples are already uploaded to the OSD App Server. Yellow samples are not uploaded yet. Red samples indicate that mandatory information is missing and has to be added before you can upload the data. Mandatory fields are marked with red stars.

Upload Sample

When you hit the 'Upload Sample' button all your collected data will be uploaded to the OSD App Server. The data cannot be uploaded if mandatory information is missing. Mandatory fields are marked with red stars.
Please note that photos are not uploaded. When you are in the field your internet connection might be unstable and you might have low bandwidth which makes upload of large photos rather difficult. Therefore, we separated 'Upload Sample' from 'Upload Photos' . Now you can upload your collected data directly and later on, when you have a better internet connection you can upload your photos hitting the 'Upload Photos' button.

Upload Photos

By hitting 'Upload Photos' both the sample data and the photos you took will be uploaded to the OSD App Server.

FAQ

When I upload data, why Do I have to have an account and log-in to megx.net?

Currently, megx.net is hosting the OSD App Server. Most importantly, it is your data and we would like to correctly link the data to you. We also need you to log-in in order to maintain security and data quality and avoid misuse of our services. However, for your convenience you can also log-in to megx.net using your Facebook, Google, Twitter or OpenID account avoiding to create yet another account.

What happens with my data I send to you?

The data will be stored in our database and made publicly available to everyone via our OSD App Server.

Where can I get more information on how to sample for the OSD?

You can find further information on the OSD webpage http://www.my-osd.org. It hosts tutorials and detailed information about the sampling process.

My GPS does not work! How can I get the geographic coordinates of my sampling point?

Currently, please use an external such map application as e.g. Google Maps. There, you can approximate your geographic location of your data collection with the mouse and do a right click. A little popup menu will appear, select 'What's here?' and the coordinates will appear in the Google search box on top of the website. The first number is the Latitude and the second the Longitude. You can then enter these latitude and longitude values in your OSD App.

What kind of photo should I take?

Any photo which gives a good impression about the location is valuable. You may want to take a photo of your sampling spot or of your kids taking the sample.... However you like
